Rugby is a high-intensity team sport played with an oval ball, where two teams of 7 or 15 players aim to score points by carrying, passing, or kicking the ball into the opponent's goal area. Players can only pass the ball backward or laterally, while tackles and rucking (physical contests) are key parts of the game. Points are scored through tries (touching the ball down in the opponentâ€™s goal area), conversions, penalties, or drop goals. Known for its physicality and teamwork, rugby is popular worldwide, particularly in countries like New Zealand, England, South Africa, and Australia.
